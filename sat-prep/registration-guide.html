SAT Registration Guide

A complete step-by-step guide to registering for the digital SAT — including deadlines, fees, document requirements, and a test day checklist.

How to Register

Registering for the digital SAT is done entirely online through your College Board account. Follow these steps to complete your registration.

1

Create a College Board Account

Go to collegeboard.org and create a free account. You will need your full legal name, date of birth, and a valid email address. Make sure your name matches your government-issued ID exactly.

2

Provide Personal Information

Fill in your high school information, grade level, and demographic details. You will also need to upload a recognizable photo (passport-style) that will appear on your admission ticket.

3

Select Your Test Date and Center

Choose an available test date from the schedule and select a test center near you. Test centers fill up quickly, so register early to secure your preferred location. The search tool on the College Board website shows available seats in real time.

4

Pay the Registration Fee

Pay the SAT registration fee using a credit card, debit card, or PayPal. As of 2026, the standard fee is $60 for domestic test-takers, plus regional fees for international students. Fee waivers are available for qualifying low-income students.

5

Download Bluebook and Complete Setup

After registering, download the Bluebook testing app on your device. College Board will prompt you to complete a test preview and run a device check to ensure your equipment is compatible. Complete these steps at least a week before test day.

Tip: Register at least 4-6 weeks before your preferred test date to avoid late fees and ensure seat availability. Late registration carries an additional fee.

For International Students

Students outside the United States can take the digital SAT at international test centers. The test format is identical to the US version.

  • Find a test center: Use the SAT Test Center Search on the College Board website to find centers in your country.
  • Fees vary by region: International registration fees include a regional fee on top of the base $60 fee. Check the fee schedule for your country.
  • Document requirements: International students must present a valid passport at the test center. Some countries may require additional identification.
  • Device requirements: If you are testing at a center that does not provide devices, you must bring your own laptop or tablet with Bluebook installed.
  • Fee waivers: Fee waivers are available for qualifying low-income international students, though the process varies by region. Contact your school counselor or local College Board representative for details.

Required Documents

On test day, you must bring the following documents. Missing any of these may prevent you from testing.

  • Admission ticket: Print or show your admission ticket from your College Board account. It contains your test center details and personal information.
  • Valid photo ID: A government-issued ID (passport, driver's license, or national ID card) that matches the name you used during registration. School IDs are not accepted.
  • Testing device: Your laptop or tablet with Bluebook installed, fully charged. Bring your charging cable as well.
  • Approved calculator (optional): If you plan to bring your own calculator, ensure it is on the approved list.
  • Snacks and water: For the 10-minute break between sections.

Upcoming Test Dates

The SAT is offered seven times per year in the United States: August, October, November, December, March, May, and June. International test dates may vary slightly.

Check the official College Board SAT dates page for the most up-to-date schedule, registration deadlines, and score release dates for your region. Registration typically opens about 6-8 weeks before each test date.

Note: Test dates and deadlines are subject to change. Always verify on the official College Board website before making travel or study plans around a specific test date.

Test Day Checklist

Use this checklist to make sure you are fully prepared on the morning of the test.

  • Fully charged device with Bluebook installed and updated
  • Charging cable and power bank (if available)
  • Admission ticket (printed or digital)
  • Valid government-issued photo ID
  • Approved calculator (with fresh batteries if battery-powered)
  • Snacks and water for the break
  • Watch (without internet connectivity) for pacing
  • A good night's sleep and a healthy breakfast
  • Arrive at the test center by 7:45 AM (doors close at 8 AM)
Final Tip: The night before, confirm your test center address, set multiple alarms, and lay out everything you need. Being organized reduces stress and helps you focus on doing your best.

For full details, visit the College Board test day page.

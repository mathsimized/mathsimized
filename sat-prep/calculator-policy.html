SAT Calculator Policy

Everything you need to know about calculators on the digital SAT — which models are allowed, how Desmos works, and strategies to use them effectively.

Allowed Calculators

The digital SAT allows most graphing, scientific, and four-function calculators. However, calculators with certain features (like computer algebra systems or internet connectivity) are prohibited. Below is a breakdown of approved categories.

✅ Graphing Calculators

Most popular graphing calculators are allowed, including the TI-84 series, TI-Nspire (non-CAS), Casio fx-9750/9860 series, and HP Prime (non-CAS). These are the most powerful option for SAT Math.

✅ Scientific Calculators

Any scientific calculator without graphing capabilities is allowed. Examples include the TI-30 series, Casio fx-260/300 series, and most standard scientific models. These are sufficient for most SAT Math questions.

✅ Four-Function Calculators

Basic four-function calculators (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) are allowed. However, they are rarely sufficient and are not recommended for the digital SAT Math section.

❌ Prohibited Calculators

Calculators with computer algebra systems (CAS) like the TI-Nspire CAS, TI-89 series, TI-92 series, HP 50g, and any calculator with a QWERTY keyboard, internet access, or audio/video recording capability are not allowed.

For the complete list of approved and prohibited calculators, visit the official College Board calculator policy page.

Desmos Built-in Calculator

The digital SAT includes a built-in Desmos graphing calculator available for all 44 questions in the Math section. This is one of the biggest changes from the paper SAT.

  • Available in both modules of the Math section.
  • No need to bring your own calculator — though you still can if you prefer.
  • Fully integrated into the Bluebook testing app — no switching between applications.
  • Can graph equations, create tables, perform regressions, and more.
  • Works the same as the free online Desmos calculator at desmos.com.
Tip: Practice with Desmos during your SAT prep so you are comfortable with its features on test day. Take our Desmos Guide for a complete walkthrough.

Calculator Tips for SAT Math

  • Use Desmos for graphing questions: If a question involves a function or equation, graph it in Desmos to visualize the relationship immediately.
  • Check solutions: After solving an equation algebraically, plug your answer into Desmos to verify it is correct.
  • Know when not to use a calculator: Some questions are faster to solve by hand or through reasoning. Do not reach for the calculator automatically — assess each question first.
  • Bring your own backup: Even though Desmos is built-in, bring an approved calculator as a backup in case of technical issues.
  • Master regression: For data analysis questions, use Desmos's regression feature to find the line or curve of best fit quickly.
  • Use sliders for parameter questions: When a question involves variable parameters, use Desmos sliders to explore how changes affect the graph.

Common Mistakes

  • Assuming your personal calculator is identical to Desmos: Different calculators have different interfaces and capabilities. If you bring your own, make sure you know how to use it for the specific operations you will need.
  • Forgetting to clear your calculator: On test day, ensure your personal calculator's memory is cleared if required by your test center.
  • Over-relying on the calculator: The calculator is a tool for verification and visualization, not a substitute for understanding the underlying math.
  • Not practicing with the built-in Desmos: Since Desmos is always available, practice using it in your prep. Trying to learn it on test day will cost you valuable time.
  • Using an unapproved calculator: Show up with a prohibited model and you will not be allowed to use it. Check the College Board list before test day.

Official Rules

  • You may use a calculator only during the Math section. Calculators are not allowed during the Reading and Writing section.
  • You may bring up to two approved calculators to the test center.
  • Sharing calculators between students is not permitted.
  • Your calculator must not have internet access, Bluetooth, or any wireless communication capability.
  • If your calculator has a cover or case, you must remove it before the test begins.
  • You cannot use a calculator that makes noise (like a talking calculator).
  • The test proctor may inspect your calculator at any time during the test.
  • For the complete policy, visit collegeboard.org.
